Jina AI's r.jina.ai reader uses HTTP fetching, not a real browser. It is fast precisely because it skips JavaScript execution. That works fine for static sites, news articles, and documentation. But roughly a third of the modern web requires JavaScript to render its content, and an even larger share uses Cloudflare or similar protection that fingerprints the client.

WHEN TO USE JINA

Jina AI Reader is a good fit when

  • You are scraping static content: news, Wikipedia, documentation, blogs
  • Speed matters more than reliability (1.4s vs 2.1s)
  • You have a very high volume of simple URLs and want to keep costs at zero
  • You do not need MCP integration or bot-bypass capabilities

WHEN TO USE ANYBROWSE

anybrowse is the right choice when

  • Your agent needs to fetch arbitrary URLs including SPAs and Cloudflare sites
  • You are building an MCP-integrated tool for Claude, Cursor, or Windsurf
  • You need reliable results, not just fast results on easy sites
  • You want pay-per-use pricing without a subscription
  • You need batch processing or structured extraction

The common pattern

Many developers start with Jina for its free unlimited tier, then switch to anybrowse when they hit their first wall: a React dashboard that renders blank, a job listing behind Cloudflare, or an e-commerce product page that requires cookie consent. anybrowse handles all of these because it runs a real browser.
