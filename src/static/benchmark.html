KEY FINDINGS

Where the differences actually matter

For simple static pages and documentation sites, all three APIs perform similarly. The gap widens significantly once you need to scrape modern JavaScript applications, e-commerce sites with anti-bot measures, or any domain behind Cloudflare.

Jina AI is a good starting point for free, fast scraping of public content. But at 23% on Cloudflare sites and 41% on JS-heavy apps, it is not reliable enough for production AI agents that need to ingest arbitrary URLs.

Firecrawl performs reasonably well but requires a paid subscription from day one for production use, and at $16/month minimum it adds meaningful fixed costs before you have validated your product.

anybrowse uses a real headless Chromium browser with fingerprint spoofing and optional residential proxy fallback. That is why it holds 71% on Cloudflare sites where Jina collapses to 23%. The tradeoff is 2.1s average latency versus Jina's 1.4s, which is acceptable for most agent workloads.
